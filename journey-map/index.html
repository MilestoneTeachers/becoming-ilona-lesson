Becoming Ilona

Judy's Wartime Path, 1937 to 1949

A teaching map

One child. Six places. One name change.

Judy Abrams, born Judit Grünfeld, lived under three names before she turned ten. This map traces her geography from her birth in Budapest in 1937 to the publication of her memoir in 2011. Scroll to follow each step.

Built as a classroom companion for Tenuous Threads, published by the Azrieli Foundation Holocaust Survivor Memoirs Program.

Scroll to begin.

What this map shows

Mapping a survivor's geography turns history from a date on a page into a place a child can stand near. Judy lived these six points before she turned ten, and the names on the map, Stefánia Street and Pincehely and Pier 21 and Mile End, are still where they were.

Read more about the survivor memoirs program at the Azrieli Foundation: memoirs.azrielifoundation.org.
