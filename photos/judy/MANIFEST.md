## Photos NOT delivered, and why

### Of Judy Abrams the person (still no license-clean source)
**Result unchanged from pass 1:** No Wikimedia, no public CC source. Brandon would need direct Azrieli Foundation permission for any photograph of Judy.

### Period photos of Hungarian Jewish family meals or synagogue interiors (1930s)
**Searched:** Wikimedia Commons (`Hungarian Jews 1930s`, `Budapest Jewish children`, `Fortepan család 1938`).
**Result:** Returned mostly Nazi persecution materials and Berlin-1933 imagery. The two Fortepan family-keyword results were a family crypt and a church called "Holy Family." No domestic family-meal scenes surfaced under explicit license.
**Decision:** Not delivered. The Budapest park 1938 and ice-skating 1938 photos serve the "warm pre-war life" purpose without overlapping with darker imagery.

### Ursuline-specific Hungarian convent imagery
**Searched:** Wikimedia Commons (`Ursulines Budapest`, `Ursuline convent`, `Hungarian nuns`).
**Result:** Zero Hungarian-Ursuline-specific photos surfaced under explicit license. The Pannonhalma Benedictine 1943 photo is the closest available — Hungarian, wartime, religious women in habits — but it is a different order. **Caption MUST clarify the order distinction.**
**Decision:** Pannonhalma photo delivered with explicit caption guidance in the sidecar JSON.

### Hungarian railway / journey context for Page 7 journey-map
**Searched:** Wikimedia Commons (`Hungarian railway 1940`, `Fortepan Pincehely`).
**Result:** Some 1940s Hungarian railway station photos exist but they are mostly post-1944 wartime infrastructure photographs that may carry darker associations. Did not download.
**Decision:** Not delivered. The Pincehely aerial + Pier 21 photos already serve the journey-map page strongly without needing a railway photo.

### Pre-war Hungarian synagogue interiors
**Searched:** Wikimedia Commons (`synagogue Budapest`, `Hungarian synagogue 1930s`).
**Result:** Most synagogue photos are either contemporary (post-1990) or postwar memorial. Pre-war synagogue interiors with clear license + Hungarian provenance did not surface in this pass.
**Decision:** Not delivered. Brandon may want to do a focused follow-up if a synagogue interior is critical to a specific page.

### Postwar Hungarian Jewish life / DP camps (1945-1949)
**Searched:** Wikimedia Commons (`displaced persons camp 1947`, `Holocaust children rescue`).
**Result:** A 49-image series of the **Barletta DP Camp 1947** exists under CC BY-SA 3.0 (verified). Did not download a Barletta image because the Pier 21 1948 photos are a stronger pedagogical fit (Canadian arrival is what Judy actually did) and the lesson is a Canadian Grade 6 product. If Brandon wants European-DP-camp imagery for an additional contextual page, the Barletta series is available under CC BY-SA 3.0 from photographer Shalom Strassberg, hosted at Wikimedia Commons.
**Decision:** Not delivered (but is a known available source if needed later).
